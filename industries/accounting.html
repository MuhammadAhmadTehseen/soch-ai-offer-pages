Curriculum — weeks 2 to 4

Built for accounting & fintech. Run on your real work.

WEEK 2 · DEPARTMENT SKILL LABS

Each team gets a Claude Project tuned to their work

The team splits by function — bookkeeping/ops first, then onboarding/KYC, then reporting. Each track configures a Claude Project with their firm's templates, chart of accounts, and standard email language. Labs run on your real (anonymised where needed) client files so the output can be judged against actual quality standards.

Document intake & extraction

Invoices, receipts and statements parsed into structured fields and written to the client ledger sheet. AI extracts and flags exceptions — the accountant reviews and posts. AI never posts entries directly.

Client communication desk

Claude Project loaded with the firm's tone and client context: chase emails, clarification requests and onboarding replies drafted in Estonian, English or Russian — reviewed before sending.

Meeting notes → client update

Client call notes or recordings pasted in → structured summary, open items list, and draft follow-up email. The accountant sends; Claude assembles.

Onboarding & KYC pack

For e-residency and international client firms: intake forms summarised, document completeness checked, compliance file assembled for human sign-off — AML rules stay with the accountant.

On reconciliation and regulated outputs — the hard line

AI assists; the accountant signs off. Claude can summarise ledger exceptions, flag reconciliation gaps, and draft the monthly report narrative — but it never posts entries, never certifies figures, and never produces a statutory output without a qualified accountant reviewing the numbers against source records. This rule is installed as firm policy in Week 2 and documented in the AI use policy.

Why this matters: Claude does not have access to your accounting software in real time, cannot guarantee numeric accuracy, and can state incorrect figures with confidence. Every workflow includes a human verification step for any number that will appear in a client-facing or regulatory document.

Skills + Cowork What your teams build — and own

In the labs, each function builds reusable Claude Skills and Cowork workflows on its own client files. No coding required — a Skill is a plain-language file that captures how your firm wants the job done, and Cowork runs the multi-step version on your files:

  • Document intake: a folder of invoices and statements → fields extracted → client sheet updated, exceptions flagged for the accountant, run as one Cowork task
  • A missing-documents Skill that spots gaps in a client file and drafts the polite chase email for one-click send
  • A month-end reporting Skill that turns the period's figures into a client-ready report in your template — numbers verified by the accountant
  • An onboarding Skill that assembles the folder structure, KYC checklist and welcome sequence for a new client
WEEK 3 · REAL-WORK BRIEF

The week the skill becomes yours

Each accountant runs one real client month-end (or one full onboarding) through the new setup during the normal work week, logging minutes per file before and after. The review session tunes the extraction prompts and exception rules to your chart of accounts.

WEEK 4 · ROLLOUT & DEMO DAY

Owned, measured, handed over

Each function demos what it built. Named owner per workflow, one metric each — minutes per client file, documents processed per day, chase emails sent without human drafting. Handover pack: prompts, Claude Projects, Skills, Cowork workflows and templates, documented in-house.
