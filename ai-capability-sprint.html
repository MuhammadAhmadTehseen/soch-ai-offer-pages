Week 1 · Foundation Day

Everyone set up.
Before we leave the building.

The foundation day is the same across all industries and firm sizes. By the end of 3.5 hours on-site, every person in the session has:

  • Claude.ai Team account set up and configured
  • Claude Projects loaded with the firm's templates, tone guide and SOPs
  • Google Workspace fully connected (Gmail, Drive, Sheets, Calendar)
  • Claude Cowork installed and delegating its first real task on your files
  • Data-handling walkthrough and hallucination protocol covered
  • Firm's one-page AI use policy reviewed and adopted
  • Clear next step per person going into Week 2
Week 2 · Skill Labs

Learn to use Claude well.
Then build your own.

The firm splits into department tracks. Each track focuses first on using Claude well — prompting precisely, building Claude Projects with the right context — then builds its own capability: reusable Skills that capture the team's output formats, tone and templates, and Cowork workflows that run the repetitive multi-step jobs. Everything is theirs to keep and extend. What's covered depends on the industry curriculum:

Law firms

Drafting engine on your templates + precedents, document review, research memo protocol, insolvency filing workflow.

Accounting & fintech

Document intake & classification, client communication desk, month-end close assistant, onboarding & KYC pack builder.

HR & recruitment

CV screening against your scorecards, interview-note synthesis, outreach in each consultant's voice, market mapping & longlists.

Marketing & creative

Tone-of-voice engine per client, brief and proposal scaffolding, self-writing client reports, content variant production at scale.

Week 3 · Real-Work Brief

The week the skill
becomes yours.

This is the week that separates a sprint from a workshop. Each person applies everything from Week 2 to one real client deliverable during a normal working week — with time logged before and after.

Pick one real thing

Not a practice exercise. A live deliverable: a pleading, a month-end report, a role shortlist, a campaign variant pack. Real output, real deadline.

Log the time

Before-and-after time per task. The number that comes out of this week is the metric each team tracks from month one — set against a real baseline.

Review and tune

The review session goes through what worked, what didn't, and where the workflow or prompt needs adjusting. Nothing ships to clients without sign-off.

Week 4 · Demo Day & Handover

handed over.
