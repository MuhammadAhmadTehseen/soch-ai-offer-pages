Honest answer

Where the human stays in charge — always.

Every firm asks this before they commit. These aren't caveats — they're the constraints that shape how we design every workflow.

Claude gets things wrong — confidently

Hallucination is documented, consistent, and not going away. Claude fabricates case citations, states incorrect figures, and produces plausible-sounding errors with no uncertainty. Every workflow we build includes a human verification step for any factual output. This is a design constraint, not a footnote.

Judgment stays with the professional

Client strategy, ethical calls, complex negotiations, creative direction — Claude doesn't make these decisions. It writes the draft so the lawyer focuses on the argument. It screens the CVs so the recruiter focuses on the conversation. The professional decides; Claude does the typing.

No live data, no firm memory (by default)

Claude has no access to the internet, your live systems, or anything outside what you give it in the session. Claude Projects solves the firm-context problem — but each session still starts from what you provide. Workflows are designed around this so your team isn't surprised by it.

Large files need staged processing

Claude can handle long documents — but not a 400-page case bundle or a year's worth of inbox in one go. Automations are built to process in stages: chunk, summarise, escalate. We build this into the workflow so your team doesn't have to think about it.

Common questions

Everything you're wondering about.

Is this a course, or do you actually set things up?+
We set things up. Claude is installed on every device and connected to your Google Workspace during the foundation day — the teaching happens on your live setup, not slides. You end day one with working tools, not homework.
How big does our firm need to be?+
The Setup Day fits firms of 1–50 people; the Capability Sprint fits firms of up to 100 with two or more distinct functions. On-site sessions hold up to 30 people — larger firms split into two sessions.
What do we need before you arrive?+
Google Workspace (Drive, Sheets, Gmail, Calendar) and a decision-maker in the room. That's it — we handle installation, configuration and accounts on the day.
Is it fully on-site?+
The foundation day is on-site in Tallinn, led by Rizwan. In the Sprint, weeks 2–4 run remotely — live build labs, an async brief, and a demo day — with Soch's technical team delivering throughout.
What happens if someone misses a session?+
Sprint sessions are structured so departments work in parallel — a missed person catches up inside their department's track, and the week-3 brief is async by design.
What about our client data?+
Everything runs inside your own Google Workspace and your own Claude accounts — Soch doesn't take copies of your files. On Anthropic's paid plans (Team and above), your conversations are not used to train their models. We'll walk your decision-maker through the data-handling setup before anything is connected.
We run on Microsoft 365, not Google Workspace. Can this still work?+
Yes — Claude.ai Team and Claude Projects work independently of your file system. The automations we build in n8n can connect to Microsoft 365 (Outlook, OneDrive, SharePoint) as well as Google Workspace. The setup day takes slightly longer for M365 firms; we'll confirm this on the scoping call.
What does it cost to run after the programme ends?+
Claude.ai Team is ~$25/user/month, billed directly by Anthropic. n8n Cloud starts at ~$20/month for the firm's automation workspace. These are your only ongoing costs — there's no Soch retainer, and the automations run without us once the handover is done.
What happens after the program ends?+
Every automation has a named internal owner and a metric, and your team keeps the full handover pack — prompts, Claude Projects, n8n workflow files, and the AI use policy. Firms that want to go further scope that as a follow-on engagement.
